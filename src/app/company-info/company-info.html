Dos and Don'ts for RCS

Do's:

  1. Comply with TRAI & Google Policies:

    Adhere to the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) guidelines for messaging, and also follow Google's RCS Business Messaging policies, especially regarding spam, data privacy, and consent.

  2. Register on Google's Verified Sender Program:

    Ensure that your business is registered as a verified sender through Google to build trust with your customers. This helps in brand recognition and improves message delivery rates.

  3. Use Google's Verified Business Messaging Features:

    Leverage features such as branding, chatbots, quick replies, rich media (images, videos), and carousels. These features make your messages more engaging and interactive, leading to higher conversion rates.

  4. Obtain Customer Consent:

    Ensure you have explicit opt-in from customers before sending any RCS messages. Maintain proper records of consent for compliance.

  5. Focus on Personalization:

    Personalize messages based on user preferences, purchase history, and behavior to enhance engagement. Google's RCS platform allows you to create tailored content, so make use of this functionality.

  6. Use Rich Media Wisely:

    Use images, videos, and buttons to create interactive experiences. However, ensure that media enhances the message rather than overcomplicating it.

  7. Provide Opt-Out Options:

    Always offer an easy-to-access opt-out mechanism in every message to allow customers to stop receiving messages if they wish.

  8. Ensure Message Security:

    Google's RCS Business Messaging supports encryption for secure communication. Use this feature, especially when transmitting sensitive information such as OTPs, personal data, or payment details.

  9. Test Your Messages:

    Perform comprehensive testing across different devices and networks to ensure that your messages (including media, buttons, and chat flows) work properly.

  10. Track and Analyze Performance:

    Use Google's analytics tools or integrate with your internal CRM to monitor metrics such as read receipts, click-through rates, and user engagement. This data can help improve future campaigns.

Don'ts:

  1. Don't Spam:

    Avoid sending unsolicited or frequent messages to users. Over-messaging can lead to opt-outs and even regulatory issues which may impact your BOT and leads to temporary suspension.

  2. Avoid Generic, Non-Interactive Messaging:

    Don't treat RCS like traditional SMS. Avoid sending static, text-heavy messages. RCS offers rich, interactive features‚Äîutilize them to create meaningful engagements.

  3. Don't Violate User Privacy:

    Never misuse user data or send messages without proper consent. Adhere to data privacy laws, such as the Personal Data Protection Bill in India, to avoid legal issues.

  4. Avoid Sending Messages at Odd Hours:

    Be mindful of the time when sending messages. Avoid sending promotional messages late at night or early in the morning unless it's crucial or user-initiated.

  5. Don't Overload with Media:

    While rich media is a great feature of RCS, avoid excessive use of images, videos, or carousels that may slow down delivery or overwhelm the user.

  6. Don't Ignore Customer Support Features:

    Google's RCS Business Messaging allows for two-way communication. Don't ignore customer queries or fail to provide real-time support where necessary.

  7. Don't Skip Message Localization:

    Avoid sending messages only in English if your audience is diverse. RCS supports localization, so take advantage of this by sending messages in the local language for better engagement.

  8. Don't Send Long, Complex Messages:

    Keep your messages concise and easy to understand. Avoid sending long paragraphs that could lead to disengagement.

  9. Don't Forget Branding Consistency:

    Always maintain consistency in your branding. Since RCS allows custom logos, colors, and chat themes, use these features to reinforce your brand identity in every communication.

  10. Don't Overlook Compliance Updates:

    Regularly update yourself with any changes in Google's RCS Business Messaging guidelines and Indian regulations to ensure your campaigns are always compliant.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is RCS Business Messaging?

    RCS (Rich Communication Services) is an upgraded SMS channel that enables branded, interactive messaging on Android devices.

  2. How is RCS better than SMS?

    RCS supports rich media (images, videos, carousels), branding, verified sender info, and two-way conversational messaging.

  3. Who can use RCS Business Messaging?

    Brands across industries can use RCS for marketing, customer service, updates, and transactional messages.

  4. Is RCS available on all phones?

    RCS works on Android devices with RCS-enabled carriers and messaging apps like Google Messages.

  5. Do customers need an app to receive RCS messages?

    No, RCS messages are delivered directly to the native messaging app on compatible Android phones.

  6. How are RCS messages delivered?

    Messages are delivered over mobile data or Wi-Fi, not traditional SMS channels.

  7. What content types does RCS support?

    RCS supports text, images, videos, buttons, carousels, suggested replies, and more.

  8. Is RCS secure?

    Yes, RCS includes verified sender IDs and encryption between carrier and device (not end-to-end).

  9. What is required to start RCS messaging?

    Brands need to register an RCS agent, provide brand assets, and work with an approved messaging partner.

  10. Can we track performance on RCS?

    Yes, RCS provides read receipts, delivery reports, and interaction tracking.

  11. What are the pricing models for RCS?

    Pricing varies by region and carrier, commonly based on cost per-message or session-based models.

  12. Can RCS support two-way conversations?

    Yes, RCS enables real-time, interactive two-way messaging with users.

  13. Is RCS available globally?

    RCS availability varies by market and carrier; it is widely supported in the US, UK, India, and parts of Europe.

  14. What are the limitations of RCS?

    It's not supported on iOS, and reach depends on carrier and device compatibility in India.

  15. Can RCS be integrated with CRMs or bots?

    Yes, RCS can be integrated with CRMs, AI chatbots, and automation platforms via APIs.
